📌 Key Concepts

Reducing Balance Method

Interest is charged monthly on the remaining principal. As principal falls, so does the interest component — while EMI stays constant. Early months = more interest; later months = more principal.

Moratorium / Repayment Holiday

During moratorium, only interest is paid. The principal is completely unchanged. This lowers initial cash outflow but means Phase-2 EMI is calculated on the full original principal.

EMI — Equated Monthly Instalment

Fixed monthly payment by the annuity formula: EMI = P × r / (1 − (1+r)^−n). The EMI never changes; the split between interest and principal shifts every month.

Effective Cost of Borrowing

Total Interest ÷ Principal × 100. For ELGS 5.0 at 8.75% with 1-yr moratorium this is ~26–28% — the true price of 5 years of capital access beyond just the nominal rate.

Prepayment Benefit

Any lump-sum payment reduces outstanding principal. Future interest is calculated on this lower base, so even a small prepayment creates compounding savings over the remaining tenure.

Interest vs Principal Over Time

In early EMI months, most payment is interest. Over time, as principal shrinks, the interest share shrinks and principal share grows — even though the EMI amount stays identical every month.
